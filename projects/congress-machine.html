Congress Machine

Keeping up with Congress is one of the most important and most difficult parts of being a citizen in the United States. Congress Machine is trying to make that easier.

Post / 2026-03-30T22:13

CongressMachine.com is a data-driven legislative tracking dashboard designed to monitor the activities of the United States Congress in real-time. It functions as an aggregator that pulls together various streams of political data into a single interface.

It tracks thousands of pieces of legislation (currently showing over 14,000 bills) across both the House and Senate. You can browse bills by policy area (e.g., Taxation, Health, Environmental Protection) or by specific status.

Congress Machine site highlights newly introduced bills and recently updated legislation, providing summaries and identifying the primary sponsors. It identifies specific representatives (likely based on your location or interests) and provides quick access to their profiles. The platform tracks active members of Congress (over 500) and provides a breakdown of the various committees they serve on.

One of the more distinct features is a "Recent Member Videos" section. It aggregates floor speeches, interviews, and social media clips from members of Congress (e.g., recent clips from Hakeem Jeffries, Bernie Sanders, or John Cornyn) so users can see what representatives are saying publicly.

Congress Machine site includes a section for "Tracked Funding" (currently showing over $868 million), which monitors PAC and party contributions. This allows users to see the financial distribution and contributors behind different political actors.

It is an analytical tool meant for researchers, journalists, or politically engaged citizens to follow the movement of money, legislation, and public messaging within the U.S. federal government.
